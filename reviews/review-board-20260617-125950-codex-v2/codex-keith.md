**1. One-Sentence Summary**

v2 uses feminization policy as a progressive teaching case for corpus DiD: start with a Swiss French vs France 2x2, then scale to staggered and trilingual designs, while separating population use from edited-standard use and treating composition bias `b` as the main object of diagnosis.

**2. Does v2 Resolve My Round-3 Concern?**

**Partly.** The policy-time vs calendar-time reframing is the right move: it stops pretending the ideology wave is background noise and turns it into the contrast to be diagnosed. The progressive build is also pedagogically strong. But in my lane, the problem is relocated rather than fully solved: parallel trends in `b` is only falsifiable for the parts of `b` you actually measure, while the editing operator, outcome coding invariance, and cue-induced sample selection remain mostly unmodeled.

**3. Still Wrong Or Underspecified**

- **Parallel-trends-in-`b` is not yet falsifiable enough.** Composition-margin placebos help, but they only test observed margins: profession mix, outlet mix, register, wire share, female-referent cue mix. The missing premise is that these margins span the relevant measurement bias. They do not cover editor intervention, headline/style-guide effects, or shifts in which women get titled as `Madame N`.

- **The strategy-agnostic outcome hides a measurement-invariance assumption.** Counting “any feminine marking” assumes Swiss `-esse`, French article agreement, epicene forms, and form avoidance are exchangeable indicators of one latent feminization rate. That is a strong measurement model, not a neutral coding convenience. The paper should require form-specific secondary estimates and say when divergence invalidates the pooled ATT. Dawes 2003 claims need final source verification.

- **Partial pooling is correctly scoped, but the draft should keep it away from measurement correction.** It helps sparse Swiss cells and rare titles; it does not fix the editing operator inside `b`. If outlets or titles differ in editorial treatment, shrinkage can smooth over the very artifact the paper wants to expose. Pool nuisance dimensions, but show editing diagnostics separately.

**4. Is Rung 1 Sound As Drafted?**

**Mostly, as a teaching baseline; not yet, as a fully disciplined measurement example.** It does the important things right: clear 2x2 setup, explicit edited-press estimand, policy-time vs calendar-time contrast, interference warning, and randomization null. But it currently names the `b` problem more than it operationalizes it. Rung 1 needs a concrete placebo panel: run the same design on profession mix, outlet/wire share, `Madame` cue frequency, title prestige mix, and form-strategy mix. Otherwise “parallel trends in `b`” remains a slogan.

**5. Single Most Important Fix**

Add a rung-1 **measurement contract** before drafting the remaining rungs: for each series, state the estimand, the observed `b` proxies, the unobserved part of `b`, the placebo test, the form-specific invariance check, and the downgrade rule. Population claims should require spoken/lightly edited triangulation with a stated decision rule; press-only results should stay labeled edited-standard.

**6. Verdict**

**Draft once the central fix is made.** The design is now teachable and methodologically valuable, but the measurement layer must become operational before the later rungs inherit its assumptions.
