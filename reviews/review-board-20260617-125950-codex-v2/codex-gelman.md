**Gelman-Lane Review**

1. **One-Sentence Summary**  
v2 turns feminization into a progressive methods demonstration: simulation carries the known-truth inference, Swiss French vs France teaches the simplest DiD contrast, later rungs add staggered timing and design-choice costs, and the real cases illustrate where corpus DiD identifies, degrades, or fails.

2. **Does v2 Resolve the Round-3 Concern?**  
**Partly.** It gets the main reframing right: the paper no longer pretends staggered cross-variety DiD solves ideology waves and interference; it uses those threats as the curriculum. The partial-pooling scope is also basically correct: shrink nuisance dimensions, keep variety/treatment fixed, and do not claim pooling rescues identification.

But the fix is not complete. The design still needs a hard reporting rule for when diagnostics imply “not identified.” Otherwise Rambachan-Roth breakdown, randomization nulls, and few-cluster caveats risk becoming well-written disclaimers rather than actual constraints on claims.

3. **Still Wrong or Underspecified**

- **France as control is not yet clean enough.**  
  Inference: Swiss 1991 policy effect relative to France.  
  Missing premise: France is a valid untreated/not-yet-treated counterfactual despite the 1986 Fabius circular and shared francophone ideology/media flows. The draft says France was not “effectively” treated until 1998, but “effective” risks being outcome-defined. Treat 1986 explicitly as a placebo/null policy and define the 1991-1997 window, rather than quietly recoding France as untreated.

- **The randomization null is useful, but not yet a genuine randomization inference.**  
  Inference: finite-sample uncertainty with two varieties.  
  Missing premise: labels or treatment dates are exchangeable under a credible assignment mechanism. With two varieties, permuting labels gives a tiny reference set; shuffling years is a placebo diagnostic, not a causal p-value. In `sections/08`, “also the right inference” is too strong. Say: falsification test and calibration exercise, not powered inference.

- **Breakdown-mode sensitivity needs an explicit failure rule.**  
  Inference: whether the policy effect remains identified under plausible ideology/composition violations.  
  Missing premise: what violation magnitude counts as plausible. If the Rambachan-Roth-style breakdown value is smaller than a plausible ideology shift calibrated from external evidence, the paper must say “unidentified,” not “robustness is limited.” This should be written as a decision rule before more rungs are drafted.

4. **Is Rung 1 Sound as a Teaching Baseline?**  
**Yes, as a teaching baseline; not yet as a clean empirical baseline.** The draft does the right pedagogical work: it states the edited-standard estimand, separates population use, limits partial pooling, introduces interference, and refuses to sell the example as a feminization finding.

But three phrases need tightening before I would call it sound: “France did not endorse effectively until 1998,” “falsifiable parallel-trends assumption,” and “permutation test is also the right inference.” Those overstate the design. Add the composition-margin placebo diagnostics here, define the pre/post window, and downgrade the permutation language.

5. **Single Most Important Fix**  
Add a **diagnostic-to-claim ladder** before drafting the remaining rungs: for each diagnostic, specify what result permits an estimate, what result permits only a bounded/descriptive claim, and what result forces “not identified.” This is what makes few-cluster honesty, breakdown sensitivity, and randomization nulls real rather than cosmetic.

6. **Verdict**  
**Draft once the central fix is made.**  
The v2 architecture is good enough, but the paper needs a pre-specified rule for downgrading claims when the diagnostics fail.
