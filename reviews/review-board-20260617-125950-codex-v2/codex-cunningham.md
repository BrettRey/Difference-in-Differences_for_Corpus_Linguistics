**1. One-Sentence Summary**

v2 teaches DiD for corpus linguistics through a progressive feminization build: start with Swiss French vs France as the cleanest same-language 2x2, then add staggered francophone varieties and finally trilingual Switzerland to show how design choices change the counterfactual, threats, estimand, and diagnostics.

**2. Round-3 Concern: Partly Resolved**

Partly. v2 correctly reframes the continental ideology wave as the thing to separate from policy timing, not merely a nuisance to hand-wave away. The policy-time vs calendar-time contrast is the right teaching move.

But the concern is relocated, not eliminated. The missing premise is: absent the 1991 Swiss intervention, Suisse romande and France would have followed the same ideology-driven trajectory from 1991 to 1997. That is plausible enough for a teaching baseline, but it is not automatic. If Suisse romande adopted in 1991 because it was already on a different ideological path, then divergence at 1991 is not clean policy identification.

**3. Still Wrong Or Underspecified**

- The rung-1 control condition is overstated. Section 08 says the two varieties “share a language but not a policy,” but France had the 1986 Fabius circular. The needed inference is narrower: France is an untreated-or-ineffective-policy counterfactual for the 1991-1997 Swiss post period. Say that explicitly.

- The baseline 2x2 window needs to be nailed down. The clean teaching contrast is pre-1991 vs 1991-1997. France’s 1998 shift can be a follow-on validation or second event, but not part of the first clean 2x2 control period.

- Randomization null testing is useful as a falsification check, but with two varieties it is not “the right inference” unless you defend an assignment mechanism. With N=2, label permutation is a very coarse placebo, not a magic p-value machine.

**4. Is Rung 1 Sound As A Teaching Baseline?**

Yes, with one fix. It is the right first teaching case because it gives the reader a concrete treatment, control, outcome, counterfactual, SUTVA threat, and inference limitation before scaling up.

But as drafted, it tries to carry too much apparatus at once. The teachable spine should be: “What counterfactual makes the Swiss 1991 policy effect credible?” Then every diagnostic should map to a missing premise. Partial pooling is scoped correctly in the design spec: shrink nuisance dimensions within/conditional on varieties, keep variety/treatment effects fixed across varieties. In section 08, clarify that period pooling must not shrink away the event-time contrast itself.

**5. Single Most Important Fix**

Rewrite rung 1 around the exact identifying claim:

“Between 1991 and 1997, France functions as the counterfactual for Suisse romande only if the shared francophone ideology trend would have moved both series similarly absent the Swiss policy, and if French media interference is measured or bounded.”

That sentence gives the reader the estimand, window, control status, and missing premise.

**6. Verdict**

**Draft once the central fix is made.**

The progressive build now has a real teachable spine, but rung 1 must stop calling France simply untreated and must define the 1991-1997 identifying window before the remaining rungs inherit its logic.
