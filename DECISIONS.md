2026-06-17 - Key precedent found (literature intake): Burnett & Bonami (2019, Language in Society 48), "Linguistic prescription, ideological structure, and the actuation of linguistic changes: grammatical gender in French parliamentary debates." They already study the 1986 (Fabius) and 1998 (Jospin) feminization policies as treatments on feminine grammatical gender in French parliamentary (spoken) debate, finding NO effect in 1986 and a dramatic effect in 1998, attributed to a 1990s shift in gender ideology rather than the policy itself. POSITIONING CONSEQUENCE: the France 1986/1998 before/after is taken; our novelty must be the causal-design upgrade -- cross-variety staggered DiD (Quebec 1979 / Suisse romande 1991 / Belgium 1993 as not-yet-treated controls) plus the composition/measurement layer -- converting their within-France, ideology-explained account into an identified estimate with a control series they lacked. Must engage them directly (Rapoport). THREAT they hand us: the "treatment" (a feminization circular) is not a clean exogenous shock; its effect is ideology-moderated (identical policy, opposite outcomes in 1986 vs 1998), i.e., treatment-effect heterogeneity plus a time-varying confounder. This is the parallel-trends problem in its sharpest form and is now the paper's central identification question; it justifies the heterogeneity-robust estimators in Section 07. Corroboration: Fujimura (2005, full text) shows a sharp post-1998 jump for ministre/depute(e) and prestige/domain resistance (professeur, auteur, medecin) = composition conditioning; Flesch, Abbou & Burnett (2026, Linguistics) show ~5% generic masculine among activist speakers = sample-dependence sharpening the population-estimand caveat. Reason: intake located the closest prior art; it reshapes positioning and surfaces the central identification threat.
